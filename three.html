"In the olden days?"

Tim Berners-Lee, the inventor of the World Wide Web,
used a NeXT computer to create the first website in 1991.
This marked the beginning of the internet as we know it today.

By the early 2000s, the web evolved from a
decentralised, experimental network into a mainstream social and commercial space.
Personal homepages and artist-run servers were gradually replaced by hosted platforms such as:
Blogger, MySpace, YouTube, and later Facebook and Instagram.
